
Blood Pressure Diagnosis Guidelines:

1. **Normal Blood Pressure**:
   - Systolic (upper number): Less than 120 mm Hg
   - Diastolic (lower number): Less than 80 mm Hg

   Recommended Action:
   - Maintain a healthy lifestyle to keep your blood pressure in the normal range.

2. **Elevated Blood Pressure**:
   - Systolic: 120-129 mm Hg
   - Diastolic: Less than 80 mm Hg

   Recommended Action:
   - Adopt a healthy lifestyle, including regular physical activity, a balanced diet, and stress management.

3. **Hypertension Stage 1**:
   - Systolic: 130-139 mm Hg
   - Diastolic: 80-89 mm Hg

   Recommended Action:
   - Lifestyle changes are essential, and medication may be recommended based on risk factors for cardiovascular disease.

4. **Hypertension Stage 2**:
   - Systolic: 140 mm Hg or higher
   - Diastolic: 90 mm Hg or higher

   Recommended Action:
   - Lifestyle changes and medication are necessary to lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of complications.

5. **Hypertensive Crisis** (Consult your doctor immediately):
   - Systolic: Higher than 180 mm Hg
   - Diastolic: Higher than 120 mm Hg

   Recommended Action:
   - This is a medical emergency. Seek immediate medical attention to prevent life-threatening complications such as stroke or heart attack.
   
General Tips for Managing Blood Pressure:
- Reduce salt (sodium) intake.
- Eat a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Engage in regular physical activity.
- Limit alcohol consumption.
- Avoid smoking and manage stress.

Note: Always consult your healthcare provider for personalized advice based on your individual health conditions.
